<h2>Understanding Padel Racket Shapes</h2><p>The most important decision when buying a padel racket is choosing the right shape. There are three main shapes: round, teardrop, and diamond. Round rackets have a larger sweet spot and are more forgiving — ideal for beginners and intermediate players. Diamond-shaped rackets are harder to control but generate enormous power — favoured by advanced players with precise technique. Teardrop shapes offer a balance between control and power, making them the most popular choice for club players of all levels.</p><h2>Materials and Construction</h2><p>Modern padel rackets are made from combinations of fibreglass, carbon fibre, and EVA or FOAM rubber cores. Fibreglass faces are more flexible and forgiving, while carbon fibre offers precision and responsiveness. The core material significantly affects the feel: soft EVA foam provides a more comfortable, arm-friendly experience, while harder foam generates more direct power feedback. High-end rackets from brands like Bullpadel, Head, and Babolat use premium carbon layups and memory rubber cores for the optimal combination of performance and feel.</p>

<h2>Caring for Your Racket</h2><p>Padel rackets are more delicate than they look. Avoid hitting the ground or glass walls with the frame, store your racket in a protective case, and keep it away from extreme heat (such as a car boot in summer). A good protective tape around the frame's edge is a worthwhile investment that can extend your racket's life considerably.</p>
